The brief

Off-market. Rare. Architectural. Private.

Behind a private bridge and three twenty-four-foot gates, a three-story residence of glass, stone, and warm timber — designed by Toma Design Group across four and a half acres of agricultural estate land. What follows is the walk through.

01 / 09
The walk-through · 01

The approach

A private bridge road and three 24-foot gates. The house announces itself over cascading water before you ever reach the door.

02 / 09
The walk-through · 02

The entry

Fountains flank the axis. The view runs straight through the house to the spa rotunda — one uninterrupted line of water and timber.

03 / 09
The walk-through · 03

The atrium

A sculptural stair climbs three stories under a cascade of glass. Two passenger elevators serve every floor.

04 / 09
The walk-through · 04

The great room

Double-height living behind a wall of structural glass — part of 8,409 square feet of full impact glazing, with sliders to 25 feet wide.

05 / 09
The walk-through · 05

The kitchen

The working heart of the house, open to the morning room and the formal dining beyond — every room reading to the green.

06 / 09
The walk-through · 06

The primary suite

One of eight to nine bedrooms and sixteen baths — a full wing opening onto its own terrace and the canopy beyond.

07 / 09
The walk-through · 07

The wellness center

Sauna, steam, massage, salon, and a spa bath beneath an open oculus — with a meditation room and gym alongside.

08 / 09
The walk-through · 08

The car gallery

Eight enclosed bays below; a lift carries the collection to an air-conditioned second-floor display gallery off the lounge.

09 / 09
The walk-through · 09

The pool

A resort pool runs the length of the rear elevation. At dusk the house becomes its own lantern.
